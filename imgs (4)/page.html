Quality Assurance, or QA for short, is the systematic monitoring and evaluation of the various aspects of an educational institution
to maximize the probability that minimum standards of quality are being attained by the production process. It is the way that guarantees
that the institution has achieved its educational efficacy and adopted academic standards derived from the institutionâ€™s mission
which is defined and attained in a way commensurate with the national or international standards.

Accreditation
Accreditation is a type of quality assurance process under which services and operations of educational institutions or
programs are evaluated by an external body to determine if applicable standards are met.
What will happen if accreditation is not granted? If accreditation is not granted, graduates will face the following problems:
  • -Fewer job opportunities.
  • -Inability to gain accreditation of their degrees if they wish to accredit their degree
    in a peer accredited institution.
  • -The faculty will lose its independent structure and hence it will join another accredited entity.
