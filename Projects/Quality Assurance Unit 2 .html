FCI Fayoum
Quality Assurance Unit

  1. unit vision:


    Raising the position of the Faculty of Computers and Information - Fayoum University in order to be an accredited entity capable of achieving competitiveness at the local, regional and international levels.


  2. Unit message:


    The unit seeks to spread the culture of quality to improve the educational system and the research and service activities provided by the college in light of the standards of the National Authority for Quality Assurance of Education, in order to gain the confidence of the beneficiaries in the outcomes of the educational process in order to achieve competitiveness locally, regionally and internationally.


  3. Definition of quality assurance:


    It is the means to ensure the achievement of institutional capacity, educational effectiveness, and the application of academic standards, which derive from the mission of the concerned institution, which have been defined and achieved in accordance with the corresponding standards, whether nationally or internationally. And that the level of quality of learning opportunities, research and community participation is considered appropriate and meets the expectations of different types beneficiaries.


  4. Definition of accreditation:


    The recognition granted to an institution, if it can prove that its programs comply with the declared and approved standards and that it has systems in place to ensure quality and continuous improvement of its academic activities, in accordance with the announced controls determined by the accreditation body.


  5. What if it is not approved:


    If it is not accredited, all graduates will face the following problems:

    • Weak job opportunities in the local, regional and international market compared to graduates of accredited colleges.

    • That a graduate of an unaccredited college can prepare any academic equivalency for an international college or even a regional college. And if he desires the equivalency, he will have to prepare it through another accredited college.

    • The college's loss of its independent structure and its affiliation, in name, management, and knowledge, with an accredited College of Computers and Information.
